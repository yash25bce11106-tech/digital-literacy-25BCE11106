#PHISHING
Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs.
It usually occurs through fake emails, SMS, or websites that appear to be from trusted organizations like banks, educational institutions, or popular companies.
A typical phishing attack starts when a victim receives a message claiming urgent action is needed, such as account verification or payment confirmation.
The message contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed to look like the original.
Once the user enters their personal details, the attacker collects this information.
In some cases, attackers may also send attachments containing malware that can steal data from the user’s device.
Students, professionals, and elderly people are common targets because they may not always verify the authenticity of messages.
The consequences of phishing can include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to personal accounts.
To prevent such attacks, users should always check the sender’s email address, avoid clicking on unknown links, and enable two-factor authentication for added security.
